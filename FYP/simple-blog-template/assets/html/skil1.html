Base64

What is Base64?

Base64 is a method to encode binary data (like files or special characters) into a text format using only safe ASCII characters (A-Z, a-z, 0-9, +, /, and = for padding).

Why use it?
Safely transmit data over the internet (e.g., email attachments, web APIs).
Hide messages in CTF challenges or simple obfuscation.

Base64 uses 64 characters:

  • Uppercase letters: A to Z
  • Lowercase letters: a to z
  • Digits: 0 to 9
  • Symbols: + and /
  • Padding: = (to fill missing data)
